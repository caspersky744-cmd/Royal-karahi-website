The Royal Karahi Legacy

From a Single Karahi to a Royal Empire

In 1995, in the heart of Lahore's old city, Ustad Ghulam Rasool lit his first fire under a simple iron karahi. With nothing more than fresh tomatoes, hand-ground spices, and decades of family wisdom, he created a dish that would change the city's culinary landscape forever.

What began as a humble dhaba by the roadside has grown into Royal Karahi — a celebrated name across Pakistan, synonymous with authentic taste, premium quality, and the warmth of genuine hospitality.

Today, Ustad Ghulam's grandsons carry forward his legacy with the same recipes, the same iron karahis, and the same unyielding commitment to perfection. Every dish tells a story. Every bite carries history.

"Food is not just sustenance — it is a memory, a culture, a love letter written in spice and fire."
— Ustad Ghulam Rasool, Founder
